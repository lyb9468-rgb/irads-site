Research Platform

Research framework

Our research focuses on human-relevant immune toxicity assessment for advanced therapeutic modalities. We develop engineered tissue models, resolve immune-tissue interactions, and translate biological responses into safety-relevant decisions.

A

Framework

IRADS: Integrated Risk Assessment and Decision Strategy

IRADS is a layered research framework for connecting model construction, biological readouts, and translational interpretation. It is designed to support safety assessment when conventional models are insufficient to capture human-specific immune responses, tissue vulnerability, or modality-specific risks.

B

Ecosystem

Human-Relevant Multi-Scale Assessment Ecosystem

Our work connects multiple experimental and translational scales, including in vitro human tissue systems, in vivo benchmarking, in silico modeling, multi-organ crosstalk, immune-tissue interface analysis, and safety-relevant decision support.

C

Interface

Immune-Tissue Interface Resolution

A central aim of our research is to resolve the interface between immune activation and tissue vulnerability. We interpret toxicity as a dynamic interaction involving soluble mediators, barrier function, cellular phenotype, tissue function, imaging-derived patterns, and recovery or collapse trajectories.

D

Applications

Biotherapeutic Modality Decision Map

Advanced therapeutic modalities can produce different immune, organ, and delivery-associated risks. We use human-relevant assessment systems to compare modality-specific responses and support risk prioritization, model selection, and de-risking strategy.

E

Future-facing extensions

Emerging Directions

We also explore future-facing biological contexts where conventional safety assessment may be limited, including microgravity-related biology, physical stimulation interfaces, preservation science, and non-standard therapeutic environments. These directions are positioned as platform extensions rather than mature claims.
